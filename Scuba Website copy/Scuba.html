First Step in starting a dive career

Becoming a professional Diver

A professional diver is an individual who, along with the passion in helping other, is certified, and has met a series of qualifications, and experiences, in order to become a DiveMaster

Becoming a DiveMaster

Average Annual Salary = $35,000 - $38,000

Prerequisites

  • Open and Advanced Water Certification(recreational diving)
  • Rescue Diver Certification
  • EMR/CPR Certifications up to date
  • Log book of atleast 40 Dives

DiveMaster Course
Is the real diver bootcamp, and the first level of professional certification, and the place where you learn dive management skills. You also receive advanced in-depth academic education about the physics and physiology of diving, and are able to perfect your personal skills. Part of your training occurs in the classroom, and in the water. After the training you are expected to have the knowledge of a DiveMaster, and will be required to take an examination.

The Exam consist of
  • 800-meter swim with mask, fins, and snorkel
  • 400-meter free swim, with extended water treading
  • 100-meter rescue tow, with both you and the victim in full scuba-gear, which is all timed.

Once you have completed your DiveMaster training you are well on your way into tackling any profession that interest you. Or continue to further your training in becoming a Dive instructor, to Master Diver, which will help you gain experience,and increase your pay in these following professions.
